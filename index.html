Biography

Don Norman is a pioneer in the field of Human–Computer Interaction, known for his work on user-centered design, usability, and cognitive engineering.

Key Contributions to HCI

  • User-centered design principles
  • Design of everyday things philosophy
  • Concept of affordances in interface design
  • Seven stages of action model

Books and Publications

The Design of Everyday Things

Classic book exploring how design affects human interaction with objects and systems.

Living with Complexity

Argues that complexity is inevitable and explores how to manage it in design.
