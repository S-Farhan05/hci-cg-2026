Theoretical Contributions to HCI

Seven Stages of Action (Norman's Action Cycle)

Comprises the gulf of execution and evaluation in human-computer interaction. The stages include forming the goal, planning the action, specifying the action, executing the action, perceiving the system state, interpreting the state, and evaluating the outcome.

Concept of Affordances

How objects should suggest how they should be used in interface design. Affordances define what actions are possible and how to perform them.

User-Centered Design Principles

Framework for designing products that focus on the needs, wants, and limitations of end users.

Selected Publications

The Design of Everyday Things (1988)

Laid the foundation for understanding how design affects human interaction with objects and systems, introducing concepts like affordances and the gulfs of execution and evaluation.

The Psychology of Everyday Actions (1986)

Explored how people interact with complex systems and the cognitive processes involved in performing everyday tasks.
